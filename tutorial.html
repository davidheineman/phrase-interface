Types of Edits

You will rate edits in three forms:

  • Deletion Edits - The AI attempted to simplify by deleting unnecessary information.
  • Addition Edits - The AI attempted to add clarity by adding information which didn't previously exist.
  • Paraphrase Edits - The AI attempted to reword a complicated phrase or concept.

Think about these answers. Now let's look at why we chose each option:

Q1. Simplify wording

What the question is asking: The specific words within the edit become simpler.

Why the answer is YES: The word "creature" is easier to understand than the words "biological" and "burglars"

Q2. Simplify syntax

What the question is asking: The sentence is re-ordered such that the structure of the sentence makes it easier to understand.

Why the answer is NO: The change simply replaces the words, it doesn't affect the structure of the sentence

Q3. Simplify concepts

What the question is asking: The ideas being conveyed by the words are easier to understand.

Why the answer is YES: A "biological burglar" implies not only a creature, but also one that steals things. A "creature" is simply a creature. As you can see, information was removed from the sentence to make it easier to understand.

NOTE: This is different than Q1 (simplifying the wording of the phrase), because it simplifies the ideas being conveyed by the sentence, rather than simply replacing a phrase with a synonym.
